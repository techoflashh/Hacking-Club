Highlights of the past events

    Intro to Web Hacking

    An engaging session that covered various aspects of hacking, such as network scanning and vulnerability assessment. We provided an overview of the fundamentals of hacking and a live challenge to help participants understand how to identify and exploit vulnerabilities in computer systems. The workshop was attended by more than 80 people with significant external participation.

    RnD showcase Hacking club workshop

    During RnD Showcase 2023, we conducted a workshop on basic vulnerabilities and security threats that an individual can face at any time. The workshop included a hands-on demonstration where the participants gained first-hand experience in identifying security threats. The live demonstrations included Keylogger, IPlogger, Rubber duckies, phishing, traffic analysis, http vs https and man in the middle. The demonstration saw a footfall of approximately 100 people.

    Intro CTF for the freshers

    A club staple session that is held every year for the new batch which aims to inspire them about the world of cybersecurity and introduce them to the basics of ethical hacking. We introduce them to CTF, capture the flag events, and conduct a simple one for the freshers to try it out for themselves.

    BreakIn

    BreakIn is the Hacking Club’s flagship event which is conducted every year. It is a standard 3-day CTF with problems ranging from beginner level to advanced. All the problems are original and created by the club members themselves. The CTF consisted of approximately 20 problems featuring many categories including game hacking, cryptography, web forensics, etc. breakIn 2022 saw over 150 registrations including IIIT and non-IIIT participants.

Participation in external events

  1. UniCTF
  2. PicoCTF

    3. UniCTF organised by Hack the Box and PicoCTF by Carnegie Mellon University are competitions that cover various categories such as reverse engineering, web exploitation, cryptography, and more. Organized annually, they are one of the most prestigious and challenging competitions in the world. Our club members have participated in these events and performed wonderfully. Recently, the team secured a global rank of 72 in UniCTF 2022.
